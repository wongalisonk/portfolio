No 1 Bio

Hey there! I'm a Seattle based designer who loves to create products that are usable, useful, and beautiful. I'm currently aiming to find a home with a team that values creativity, innovation, and enthusiasm.

I am a strong advocate in user centric and data driven design. I believe in not making assumptions and the power in identifying user needs and frustrations to improve usability and enjoyment for users.

I'm also a devoted Pacific Northwesterner which means I enjoy spending my time outdoors climbing, hiking, biking, and anything else that helps me get out there and see mother nature in all her glory.

No 2 Philosophy

"Listen, respond, react"

People love to give out criticism but rarely enjoy receiving any. Something that I've had to learn in the past few years is understanding that feedback and criticism I receive on my work is not targeted towards me personally. I've learned the value of feedback and the advantages of a new perspective. So with that being said, what exactly do I mean by "listen, respond, react"?

Well the first part, listen, is straightforward, but can sometimes be difficult to do. People never to hear their work be criticized. But I've learned to listen. Like, actually listen to what people are telling me. Whether it's fellow designers or end users. Sometimes I hear input that's insightful and useful. Other times I disagree. Which brings us to respond. This is where I get to voice my opinions and ask questions. Why do you think this is better? Or, I still think my idea is better and here's why. It's the chance for me to either clarify why changes are necessary or to defend my designs. From there, we move onto reacting. This is where I use the feedback to make the necessary changes in my designs.

No 3 Motto

"Kill your babies"

It was a motto I learned in my first design class and one that has stuck with me ever since. In other others, I needed to learn how to let go of my precious and beloved ideas that I had become emotionally invested in. That didn't mean starting over every time I hit a wall or experienced a block, but understanding that even if I have invested hour (or even days) of my life on an idea, it is holding me back. I like this motto because it sounds a bit morbid, but it's memorable.
